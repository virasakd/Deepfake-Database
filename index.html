Mission

The Deepfake Incident Database is a comprehensive resource dedicated to cataloging and analyzing incidents involving Deepfakes created by artificial intelligence (AI) systems. Our database provides a centralized collection of real-world examples, allowing researchers, policymakers, and interested individuals to better understand the impact of AI technology on society.

Incident Page & Video Hosting Details

Examine our incident page for a detailed overview of each incident in our database. Users can learn more about the target audience, the timeline, the documented reports associated with it, and much more by visiting each specific incident page. Furthermore, with our most recent integrations, you can clearly examine the incident's media source.

Taxonomies

Users interested in statistical summaries of our findings can visit the Taxonomies page, which displays data through interactive pie charts. For example, through this page, users can examine categories such as system developer, system deployer, subject of matter (target), and harm type. Furthermore, for easier parsing, the interactive Taxonomies provide a direct link to a filtered collection of Deepfake incidents related to the selected category.

Deepfake Submission Page

Feel free to visit and interact with our Deepfake submission page to help our cause and expand our platform. Users can report any Deepfake incidents that they believe are noteworthy by providing at least a URL and a written description. Users can also provide a video related to the incident using our newly added "Media Address" component. Before being published on our website, all submissions will be peer-reviewed by professional editors affiliated with the Responsible AI Collaborative.
